The first blockchain consortium in the Turkish business sector

Creation of a collaboration framework for the promotion, learning and use of blockchain technology in the financial sector, enabling a permanent technological infrastructure that makes it possible to provide different use cases.

What does blockchain technology contribute to the business?

  • Cost savings
  • Improved efficiency
  • Greater transparency and traceability of information
  • Improved cybersecurity and cyber resilience
  • Greater error tolerance
  • Value exchange through digital assets

Objectives

  • To promote collaboration between businesses in order to foment blockchain projects in the all sector.
  • To improve business competition and contribute to technological development.
  • To establish mechanisms to facilitate blockchain learning and knowledge transfer.
  • To collaborate with regulatory bodies to raise awareness and validate the technological developments.
  • To develop technology that strictly complies with the regulations in force (GDPR, PSD2, EIDAS, etc.)

Digital Identity platform

Solution of the qubist consortium to verify the customer's identity faster and more securely.

Currently, when registering a new customer with a financial institution, we are challenged by a complex identification process that requires us to gather various documents to prove the identity and the activity of the customer (ID card, salary, invoices, etc.). This process is repeated at all banks, duplicating efforts and generating inefficiency.

When applying for products at a bank, the user will not have to provide any documentation if they have previously been a customer at one of the associated banks and give their consent to the sharing of their information quickly and securely.

More info

A group of companies of the consortium, together with other collaborators, is currently developing a project using blockchain technology in order to quickly and securely share a customer's identity when registering with a bank.

The aim of the project is to resolve a common problem among all financial institutions, who must verify the identity of a customer when with them for the first time. It will achieve this using a platform to enable the banks to efficiently share their identity, always pursuant to the express consent of the customer.

The development of this platform seeks to increase the efficiency of new customer registration processes by sharing documents between companies that belong to the interbank consortium qubist.

Verifying the digital identity of new customers is a common requirement among the entire sector, and the legal demands regarding this duty to know are becoming increasingly greater, with the rising costs associated with duplicate processes as well as a lower customer retention ratio during the process once the registration process has been initiated.

Therefore, much of the information that is available can be shared without affecting the competitive edge between companies and without having to resort to a trusted third party, since blockchain technology guarantees a neutral and democratic system of governance.

This platform enables the customer to take control of their data, thus, improving their user experience.

How is digital identity shared securely?

1. Creating the digital identity of a client in the blockchain network

Mustafa is registered for the first time as a client in a bank part of qubist consortium. Provides his personal data to the entity, which is registered in the blockchain network.

2. Sharing the digital identity of a common client between banks

Mustafa is registered as a client in another bank part of qubist. As his data is already registered in the blockchain network, this second entity obtains the necessary documentation for the on-boarding automatically, always getting strict consent of the interested party.

3. Add new attributes to the digital identity of a network client

Mustafa on-boards as a new client in a third entity of the blockchain network, this entity obtains the necessary information from the network but it needs new documents to complete the KYC process, this last information is requested to the client and added to its digital identity.
